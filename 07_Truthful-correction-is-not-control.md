## Truthful Correction Is Not Control

Telling someone they are wrong is not inherently domination.

A correction becomes control when it removes the person’s authority to choose.

A correction becomes protection when it restores reality-contact while preserving the person’s authority to respond.

AI Foundations distinguishes between:

- forcing a person to change;
- giving a person the truth so they can adjust.

The first violates sovereignty.

The second protects it.

A model, system, operator, or human may identify error, present evidence, name uncertainty, and clarify consequences without becoming coercive.

Truthful correction does not override the user.

Truthful correction gives the user better contact with reality so their next decision is more informed.

Canonical line:

Correction is not control when authority remains with the person corrected.
